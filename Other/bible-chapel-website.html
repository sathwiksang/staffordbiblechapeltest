Welcome & Our History

Welcome to Colonial Hills Bible Chapel

We are a warm, welcoming community of believers gathered in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ. Our chapel serves families from Stafford City and surrounding communities, offering worship services in both English and Malayalam.

As a Bible-believing chapel, we are committed to the faithful teaching and preaching of God's Word. We gather regularly for worship, prayer, fellowship, and the breaking of bread, following the New Testament pattern for the local church.

Our congregation is diverse, representing many backgrounds and cultures, but we are united in our love for Christ and our desire to grow in faith together. Whether you're seeking a church home or just visiting, we invite you to join us as we worship the Lord and study His Word.

Our Beliefs

We believe that the Bible is the inspired, inerrant, and authoritative Word of God. The sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments are fully inspired by the Holy Spirit and are our final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Each person of the Trinity is fully God, co-eternal and co-equal, yet there is one God.

We believe that Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God who became man without ceasing to be God. He was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, lived a sinless life, died on the cross for our sins, rose bodily from the dead, ascended to heaven, and will return in power and glory.

We believe that the Holy Spirit is a divine person who convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment. He indwells, seals, and empowers all believers, distributing spiritual gifts for the edification of the church and the glory of God.

We believe that salvation is by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone. It is a free gift of God, not based on human works or merit. All who truly believe in Jesus Christ are eternally secure in Him.

We believe that the church is the body of Christ, composed of all true believers. The local church is an autonomous gathering of believers who meet for worship, fellowship, teaching, prayer, and the observance of baptism and the Lord's Supper.

We believe that believer's baptism by immersion and the Lord's Supper are the two ordinances given to the church. Baptism is a testimony of faith in Christ, and the Lord's Supper is a remembrance of His death until He comes.

We believe in the personal, visible, and imminent return of our Lord Jesus Christ. He will come to receive His church and later return to establish His millennial kingdom on earth.
