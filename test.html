Our Foundation

Built upon the unchanging Word of God and the rich heritage of the Open Brethren movement

A Journey of Faith

Stafford Bible Chapel began in November 2014 through the vision of Dr. Mathew Vairamon, starting humbly in a family home and growing into a thriving community of believers. From Murphy Road to our current location at 10502 Altonbury Lane, God has been faithful in establishing this work.

As an Indian Brethren Assembly, we cherish the simplicity of New Testament Christianity. We gather without hierarchical structures, honoring the priesthood of all believers while being led by godly elders. Our worship is characterized by reverence, authenticity, and the centrality of Christ.

Located in the heart of Stafford—the vibrant center of Houston's Malayalee community— we serve families from diverse backgrounds with services in both English and Malayalam, creating a bridge between traditional faith and contemporary life.

Our Statement of Faith

The foundational biblical truths upon which our Assembly is built

We believe the Holy Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired and infallible Word of God. The Bible is completely trustworthy and is the final authority in all matters of faith and daily life.

We believe all people are sinners and in need of God's salvation. Salvation is a gift of God's grace, received through personal faith in Jesus Christ alone and not by any human works or merit. Through Christ's atoning death on the cross, His burial, and His resurrection, sinful humans can be forgiven and reconciled to God.

We believe that Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God who became fully human while remaining fully divine. Conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary, He lived a sinless life on earth. Jesus died on the cross as a perfect sacrifice for our sins, rose bodily from the dead, and ascended to heaven, where He reigns as Lord and Savior.

We believe the Holy Spirit is God at work in the world and in the believer. The Holy Spirit convicts the world of sin, gives new life to those who trust in Christ, and lives in every believer from the moment of salvation. He empowers and guides believers to live godly lives, to understand the Scriptures, and to serve Christ effectively, producing spiritual fruit in them.

We believe in the priesthood of all true believers in Jesus Christ. Every Christian has direct access to God through Jesus, our Great High Priest, and no special human priesthood or clergy class is needed. All believers are a "royal priesthood" set apart to worship God and to serve one another in love, as each is gifted by the Holy Spirit.

We believe that Jesus Christ will come again, personally and visibly, as King and Judge of all. We expect the imminent return of our Lord Jesus as He promised. At His coming there will be the resurrection of the dead and the final judgment — bringing everlasting life and joy to those who are saved, and righteous judgment to those who have rejected Christ.

We believe that the Assembly is the one spiritual Body of Christ, a living community comprising all true believers from every nation and time. Jesus Christ alone is the Head of the Assembly. Local congregations of believers meet together for worship, prayer, the teaching of God's Word, fellowship, and the observance of baptism and the Lord's Supper. The Assembly is called to unity in Christ, holiness in life, loving service, and the proclamation of the Gospel to the world.

Our Ministry Focus

Discover how we live out our faith together as a community

We Worship

Each Sunday morning we gather together and remember the death and resurrection of Christ by partaking of the bread and wine. We celebrate His great love, and count the enormous benefits we have received.

We Pray

We need Jesus's help in forgiving one another. To integrate Christian worldviews in our children's lives, we need Jesus's help. So we pray corporately that God will build His Assembly.

We Study the Bible

The Bible is God's instructional manual for life. Each Sunday we listen to a Bible lesson, and think through its implications for our individual lives.

We Enjoy Fellowship

At church we call this connection "fellowship." We are brothers and sisters, related to one another as followers of Christ. We like to have meals together for fellowship.
